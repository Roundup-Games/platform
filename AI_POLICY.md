# AI Usage Policy

The Roundup Games project welcomes AI as a tool, with clear ground rules to protect the quality of the codebase and the time of human reviewers.

## Rules

- **All AI usage must be disclosed.** State the tool you used (e.g. Claude Code, Cursor, Copilot, Amp) and the extent to which the work was AI-assisted in your pull request description.

- **The human-in-the-loop must fully understand all code.** If you cannot explain what your changes do and how they interact with the broader system without the aid of AI tools, do not submit them. "The AI wrote it and it passes tests" is not sufficient.

- **Issues and discussions may use AI assistance but must have a full human-in-the-loop.** Any content generated with AI must be reviewed *and edited* by a human before submission. AI-generated text tends to be verbose and include noise that distracts from the point. Do your research and trim it down.

- **No AI-generated media is allowed** (art, images, icons, videos, audio, etc.). Text and code are the only acceptable AI-generated content, subject to the other rules in this policy.

- **Low-effort AI contributions will be rejected.** Repeated submission of clearly AI-generated slop — code that doesn't fit the codebase conventions, ignores existing patterns, or shows no understanding of the domain — will result in all future contributions being blocked.

## Why These Rules Exist

This is a project maintained by a single human. Every pull request, issue, and discussion is read and reviewed by a person. Submitting low-effort, unreviewed AI output shifts the burden of understanding and validation from the contributor to the maintainer. That is disrespectful of maintainers' time.

AI tools are genuinely useful. Many contributors and maintainers use them productively. The issue is not the tools — it is contributors who submit AI output without understanding or curating it.

## Transparency

Roundup Games is itself developed with AI assistance. This policy is not anti-AI; it is pro-quality. The goal is ensuring every contribution represents genuine human understanding and intent.

## Scope

These rules apply to external contributions. Project maintainers may use AI tools at their discretion — they have demonstrated the judgment to use them responsibly.
